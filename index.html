Who we are

Founded in 08/15/2013 from the brave decision of Akeem Kafumba, knowing how hard a child’s life could be, we were born fighting for a healthy life with dignity to our litle ones.

The socioeconomic, educational and health conditions are major problems in the world affecting children, since many of them cannot overcome these obstacles, and don’t even have the basics to assure their existence.

After many meetings thinking about the troubles in our children’s lives, we decided to unite our strength and search for solutions to these problems, and thus the World Child Care Vision was born.

The name came by realizing that the same issues faced by the children of Africa could be found in many other places in the world. Our goal is to be worldwide.

The first step was taken with distribution of books, pens and clothes, all bought with Akeem’s own effort, and now we aim to build an organization strong enough to be the pillars that our children need to grow up.

Where we are

Our headquarters are located at Kyangwali Refugee Camp, in the Hoima district, in Uganda.

We are 287km to the northwest of the capital, Kampala.

Kyangwali hosts approximately 30,000 refugees, mostly Congolese and Sudanese.The journey from Hoima district town to Kyangwali takes more than four stressful hours on dirty bumpy roads.

The camp was established in the 1960s for Rwandan refugees but, at present, it is mainly home to Congolese refugees. The official number (in 2010, according to independent.co.ug) of refugees

from Congo, Burundi, Rwanda and Sudan is between 20,000 and 25,000 on camp, although the refugees themselves say they are over 30,000.

Uganda's healthcare performance is ranked as one of the worst in the world by the World Health Organization (WHO). Uganda is ranked 186th out of 191 nations.

Doctors and nurses generally stay in urban areas where pay and living conditions are better.

One solution to this healthcare crisis is to provide healthcare to poor rural communities and to refugees living in the Kyangwali Refugee Settlement Camp.

What We do

Vision: A healthy child that is economically and socially empowered through education and skills.

Mission: To facilitate the children’s access to health care and sustainable life through provision of knowledge and skills.

Values: Diversity, Excellence, Love, Honesty, Humanity, Integrity and Commitment.

Acting and objectives

Our goals are to provide education, health, prevent HIV/AIDS and SGBV. We care mainly for orphans and poor children who suffer with civil war and the bad living conditions of the area, but we also pay attention to widows, the elderly, the homeless and people with disabilities.

We have 2 programs, one for nutrition, aiming children between 0 and 5 years old, and an educational program, which tends to the scholar necessities of children between 3 and 18 years old.

WCCV’s objective is the empowering of children through education, leadership, health care, self reliance and entrepreneurship to change their standards of living and have a sustainable society and also:

  1. To assist orphan children in their basic needs and non basic needs such as: education, health, art and craft.
  2. Offer them medicine and proper medical treatment, through qualified doctors.
  3. Guarantee enough food and offer a balanced diet.
  4. Make sure that the children are having clean water and sanitation by maintaining the water supplies and proper hygiene.
  1. Create awareness about health promotion through the training of educators to teach people.
  2. Create love, peace and unity in society, aiming to create a one world family with no limits.
  3. Support disability, teaching people how to be self reliant.
  4. Reduce HIV/AIDS creating awareness and protection to the people, teaching them and offering blood tests.
  1. Prevent malaria, offering proper medication and giving protection to the community.
  2. To support mothers by equipping them with skills and knowledge for self-reliance.
  3. To connect needy children to existing livelihood interventions.

Our plans in every field of action are:

Education

Nursery School with 3 classrooms. For that, we’ll need to build a house big enough. We’ll also need proper tables, chairs, blackboard and chalk. We’ll have 3 teachers to whom we’ll pay a proper salary.For the elementary school, we’re planning to build 7 classrooms, for 30 students each, meaning more desks and chairs.Also, we’ll need uniforms, books and lunch for all the kids.We’ll have arts and crafting classes, teaching how to make shoes, belts, clothes and furniture, so they can have a proper job, so we’ll need every material related to these.

Health, HIV-AIDS and SGBV

We plan to create a health center where our children will be treated for free and will give support to the primary healthcare of the community. We’ll have nutritional services, assisting malnourished children with supplementary feeding.We plan to do a door-to-door survey four times per year in search of children suffering with malaria, anemia, checking their vaccination and nutritional status.We shall create awareness about HIV-AIDS and we’ll monitor the sanitation condition child to child, promoting conferences at schools and in the community, since there is a high number of kids and adults who are HIV positive.We’ll talk about SGBV in schools so the kids can grow up knowing that these troubles are obstacles for the community development and for living in Peace.We’ll organize free HIV tests, to check for the virus.Also we will give proper medication, health education and provision of insecticide treated nets, to fight malaria and cholera. We will make sure that children have clean water and sanitation by maintaining the water's source supply and proper hygiene. And we will promote health awareness through the training of educators, to help society.

Defend children’s rights
in the world

Defending their rights mean we will respect all the children rights in the world, and if someone break it, we will defend it in justice. We will work on emergencies when needed. During wars, natural disasters and diseases affecting young people. Whenever a kid might be in danger, we will be there.

Care for orphans, widows, elders, homeless and disabled people

For the orphans, we are planning to build an orphanage, where the children will be staying,eating, playing, sleeping, and having all the equipment for entertainment.For widows and the disabled we will be giving proper skills so they can support themselves, giving them a starting point to rebuild their lives.

Our Team

Akeem Kafumba

Founder-member born in Congo in 12/21/1986, comes from a background of struggling and overcoming. He suffered with the loss of his parents as a kid, due to rebel attacks. He grew up and studied in Goma, whence, years laters, in 2011, he had to run away from the violence of war, when suddenly night attacks started to target students.

He then went to Kiwaja, a village 100km from Goma, where the rebel attacks continued. He ran once again to Isasha, in the Uganda border, which was a door for the new country, and settled down in Kyangwali Refugee camp, about 300km from the capital, Kampala. Lead by his extreme experiences with poverty and danger, and with a promise and personal commitment with God, he decided to devote himself to take care of other children who may have been through similar problems.

After getting into the refugee camp, he noticed the great number of children that were not going to a school, suffering with jiggers and other diseases, and were mostly orphans. Some left their studies for the lack of school materials, clothes or even food, and for not having someone to look after them.

These same problems were found on the 14 villages that he went through. Seeing the severity of the situation, Akeem assembled a meeting with parents trying to find a collective solution, without much success, since everyone there were refugees in harsh conditions of living.

Besides all of those conflicts, there was also the lack of medicine for basic care, even in the local heath centers.

Then Akeem started to work as a supervisor in the Medics Without Borders NGO, dealing directly with children epidemic diseases. After all that and with a lot of humanitarian experience, he founded World Child Care Vision, aiming for socially and economically free children empowered by education, trying to assure a proper future to each one of them.
